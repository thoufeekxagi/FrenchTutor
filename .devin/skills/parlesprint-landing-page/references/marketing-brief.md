# ParleSprint launch brief

## Positioning

ParleSprint is a structured French-learning path for English-speaking beginners who want real French skills and eventual TCF/TEF readiness for a Canadian immigration goal.

## Core promise

Start at zero. Build useful French. Move toward exam readiness with a path that shows what to practise next.

## Primary conversion

Join the early-access list.

## Primary audience

A person who has decided that French may improve their Canadian immigration options, but does not know where to start. They need reassurance that the product begins at beginner level and covers all four skills.

## Message hierarchy

1. Real French before test tricks.
2. A visible route from beginner level to a stronger target.
3. One place for listening, speaking, reading, and writing.
4. TCF and TEF readiness as a destination, not the whole identity.
5. Launching soon; join to get first access and product updates.

## SEO intent clusters

- learn French for Canadian immigration
- TCF Canada preparation for beginners
- TEF Canada preparation for beginners
- French to NCLC 7
- French listening practice for immigration
- French speaking practice for CLB/NCLC

SEO copy must stay educational and avoid promises of immigration success, guaranteed results, or government affiliation.

## Launch funnel

Organic/search/social content -> landing page -> visitor recognizes themselves -> watches/understands route preview -> joins waitlist -> receives a clear launch update.

## First experiments

- Headline A: "Start with French. Move toward NCLC 7."
- Headline B: "French learning for the Canadian future you're building."
- CTA A: "Join the early list"
- CTA B: "Get launch updates"
- Audience qualifier: "For English-speaking beginners preparing for TCF or TEF."

## Honest proof while pre-launch

Use product previews, curriculum examples, an explicit launch status, and transparent methodology. Do not invent user counts, testimonials, score improvements, or partner logos.
