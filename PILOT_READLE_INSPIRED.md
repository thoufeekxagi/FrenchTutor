# ParleSprint — Readle-Inspired Pilot

## Purpose

Use the strongest structural lessons from Readle without copying its visual identity or pressure-based monetisation. The pilot should make ParleSprint's differentiator obvious: a learner receives one evidence-backed French mission, completes it in a coherent sequence, and uses the same language in a conversation with Marie.

This extends `PILOT_PLAN.md`; it does not replace the data, reliability, platform, or authentication work defined there.

## Current implementation status

The runtime now validates an authored A1–B2 competency framework and mission catalog at startup. `OrchestrationService` can persist a selected mission ID, scenario, prompt context, exact mission steps, and learner-facing reason inside an immutable daily `PlanSnapshot`. Every persisted `PlanTaskRecord` now also stores its modality, so a runtime executor can safely distinguish vocabulary, listening, writing, and speaking work. The Orchestration Lab uses that mission-aware plan path.

The production Today screen now reads the immutable mission plan and opens exact mission tasks through `MissionTaskExecutor`: selected vocabulary, authored listening exercises, persisted LLM-generated listening scenarios, grammar card sessions, writing tasks, pronunciation practice, and Marie roleplay. Generated roleplays are created once from the mission contract, persisted in the daily session, and reused on resume. Each completed task updates `PlanStore`; vocabulary, grammar, writing, and objectively graded listening write plan-linked evidence. Generated listening and pronunciation retain honest engagement-only completion until a reliable quality evaluator is available.

## Product decision

### The learning object is a Today’s Mission

A mission is one real, persisted learning context for the day. It connects vocabulary, grammar, listening, writing, and speaking rather than presenting those as unrelated tools.

A learner must always be able to answer:

1. What should I do next?
2. Why is it next?
3. How long will it take?
4. What evidence will be saved after I complete it?

### Screen contract: Today

- **User arrived to:** resume or begin today’s French practice.
- **They must understand:** one next mission step is ready and is selected for their goal.
- **Primary action:** start or continue the next mission step.
- **Secondary action:** skip the step for today or choose open conversation with Marie.
- **Success state:** the next stage advances and the mission progress updates from saved evidence.
- **Must not contain:** competing primary CTAs, fake scores, generic feature grids, streak-loss pressure, or an account/paywall interruption before a learner experiences value.

## Pilot scope and delivery order

## P0 — Today’s Mission shell

**Status:** in progress.

### Deliverables

- Rename the daily-plan presentation to **Today’s Mission**.
- Present the current stage as one mission step with the stage, duration, mission position, and an explicit `Chosen because` reason.
- Use `Start mission` / `Continue mission` as the single dominant CTA.
- Keep `Talk with Marie` secondary to the guided mission on Today.
- Remove test-only reset affordances from the production-facing mission surface.

### Acceptance criteria

- The first actionable card on Today has exactly one primary CTA.
- A paused stage says `Continue mission`; a pending stage says `Start mission`.
- Completion continues to update the existing pathway without fabricated progress.
- The widget remains usable at large text scale and has 44-point interactive targets.

## P0.1 — Orchestration execution bridge

The orchestration plan becomes the authority for the mission selection. `PathwayCoordinator` remains the route and stage-lifecycle owner; it must not independently select the learner’s curriculum.

### Deliverables

- Generate or load the immutable plan for the local date when Today opens.
- Resolve the first active or pending `PlanTaskRecord` to an executable pathway stage and its exact content item.
- Pass `planId` and `planTaskId` to `TaskEvidenceContext` for every task executed from the plan.
- Mark plan tasks active, completed, or skipped through `PlanStore` only after the corresponding typed stage outcome.
- Persist selected content in the daily session so interruption resumes the same plan task and material.
- Use `PlanSnapshot.explanation` and the selected task’s estimated minutes as the source for `Chosen because` and mission duration.
- Keep a safe offline fallback only when the plan contains no executable task. The UI must say why that fallback is being shown.

### Acceptance criteria

- Today never labels a generic picker as an orchestrated task unless that picker is constrained to the plan’s selected content.
- An evidence event generated by a planned task contains its `planId` and `planTaskId`.
- A task cannot be marked complete merely because a route was opened or dismissed.
- Reopening the app continues the same active plan task; it does not silently replace the plan.
- An explicit replan retires the prior immutable plan and records its reason.

## P1 — Contextual mission flow

### Deliverables

- Add a compact mission header to every pathway stage.
- Reuse the persisted daily passage/scene and vocabulary set across vocabulary, grammar, listening, writing, and Marie speaking.
- Show a quiet mission step indicator in each stage rather than independent feature navigation.
- Give every stage a completion state that says what evidence was saved.

### Acceptance criteria

- A learner can identify the mission context and step from every stage screen.
- Vocabulary introduced during a mission is visibly reused later in that same mission.
- Leaving and returning resumes the exact stage/item already persisted.

## P2 — Information architecture

### Deliverables

- Make the Path screen lead with the competency sequence: `New`, `Building`, `Ready`, and `Next`.
- Move the vocabulary constellation below the actionable competency path as an optional exploration surface.
- Reframe Practice as intentional practice and review queues, not a generic feature directory.
- Add scenario collections only where real curated content exists: TEF/TCF speaking, real-life conversations, listening, writing, vocabulary, and grammar.

### Acceptance criteria

- Path answers “what should I build next?” before it shows exploratory visualisation.
- Practice has an immediately available review or recommended action.
- No screen represents simulated readiness, scores, or recommendations as real data.

## P3 — Review and progress clarity

### Deliverables

- Expose a concrete review queue: due words, grammar repairs, and recommended practice duration.
- Use learner-facing evidence labels such as `New`, `Building`, and `Ready`.
- Summarise only real results: recalled words, completed stages, connected speaking duration, learner turns, and saved writing.

### Acceptance criteria

- Progress contains no invented fluency, pronunciation, or exam-readiness score.
- Learners can start their highest-value review within one tap from Practice or Today.

## P4 — Onboarding and conversion

### Deliverables

- Preserve the current product-first approach: goal, level, tutor choice, plan preparation, then trial conversation before account gating.
- Add notification priming only after a learner has completed useful practice or chosen a reminder time.
- Build a single calm paywall after demonstrated product value.
- State the price, renewal date, trial length, restore purchase, and limited-mode path clearly.

### Acceptance criteria

- No permission prompt appears without a contextual explanation.
- Trial and paywall claims are supported by the product.
- No repeated paywall loop, fake scarcity, streak-loss messaging, or unsupported outcome claim is introduced.

## P5 — Measurement before paid acquisition

Instrument the funnel before scaling ads:

1. `install_opened`
2. `onboarding_started`
3. `goal_selected`
4. `level_selected`
5. `tutor_selected`
6. `trial_call_started`
7. `trial_call_connected`
8. `trial_call_completed`
9. `account_gate_viewed`
10. `account_created`
11. `paywall_viewed`
12. `trial_started`
13. `subscription_started`
14. `mission_started`
15. `mission_stage_completed`
16. `mission_completed`

Primary acquisition quality metric: **cost per completed first speaking session**, not cost per install.

## Marketing implementation brief

### Positioning

> Prepare to speak French for Canada with one guided mission each day.

### Initial audience

- English-speaking TEF Canada and TCF Canada candidates.
- A1–B1 learners who can recognise French but need speaking practice.
- Learners with a Canadian immigration or exam deadline.

### Messages to test

1. `Get ready for TEF speaking with 10 minutes of guided French practice a day.`
2. `You know French words. Now practise saying them under pressure.`
3. `Stop wondering what to study next. Start one French mission chosen for your goal.`
4. A live Marie demonstration: prompt, learner attempt, repair, second attempt, saved evidence.

### Channel order

1. Apple Search Ads for TEF/TCF intent.
2. Google Search for exam and immigration searches.
3. Meta and TikTok vertical video demonstrating the live speaking product.
4. SEO pages for specific TEF speaking tasks, vocabulary, and common errors.
5. Immigration-consultant and French-tutor partnerships.

### Budget rule

Run small, separated creative and audience experiments first. Do not scale from installs alone; scale only when completed first speaking sessions and trial quality justify it.

## Non-negotiables

- Keep Inter, semantic `DesignTokens`, and existing adaptive primitives.
- One strong primary action per screen.
- 44×44-point minimum touch targets.
- Voice navigation moves a card only after one exact locally recognized command. Background noise, tutor echo, ambiguous language, imperfect transcription, and LLM intent results never navigate; physical controls remain available.
- No literal Readle branding, copy, dark-theme clone, content scraping, emoji-heavy paywall, fake social proof, streak-loss pressure, or unsupported claims.
- Preserve the existing persisted learning and orchestration behavior; visual polish must not replace real learning evidence.
